A collection of printed studies dedicated to the Moon’s cycles, geological formations, and its mythological presence across cultures. Each issue bridges scientific evidence with speculative design — merging photography, field data, and graphic composition into an ever-evolving visual record.

Each release acts as a field record — merging scientific curiosity with poetic interpretation. From eclipses to tidal rhythms, Lunarcosmic documents the beauty of quiet observation.
